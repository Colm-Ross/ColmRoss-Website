I'm Colm Ross, born June 5th 2004 in Palo Alto, California, and raised in Mountain View, I'm currently a first year Pre-Business student at the University of California Riverside, with the intention of focusing on Marketing. My love for marketing stems from my innate interest in our purchasing habits as humans, and what effects outside of the value or use of a product have on what we consume and why we consume it. I believe that our brains have been hardwired to think a certain way since we started evolving, and now with new advances in technology and the emergence of consumer culture, I am interested to see how the code of our brains can be manipulated into making different decisions. With the emergence of cell phones, and how as a society we have become dependent on them in everyday life, we have seen unprecedented amounts of new ways that we can market our products. With people always having their faces stuck in social media, we see so many more opportunities to get our products and our vision out to the consumer. An important thing to note is that when we market a product to a customer, we do not want to only market the product, but also the experience that comes with it. When you walk into an In-N-Out burger, or a Chick-Fil-A, you notice the experience that comes with getting their products is phenomenal, far superior to their fast food competitors. This is because they market you on the experience, not just the delicious food that is going to your stomach. I believe you can do this with any product, and I want to prove that with my work.
